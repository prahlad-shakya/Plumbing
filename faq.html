Frequently Asked Quetions

It’s recommended to have your plumbing system professionally inspected at least once every two years. If your home is older or you've experienced frequent issues, annual inspections are ideal.

A constantly running toilet often points to a worn flapper, misaligned float, or faulty fill valve. Check inside the tank and replace any faulty components. If unsure, contact a plumber for a quick fix.

Low water pressure can be caused by clogged pipes, a failing pressure regulator, or leaks in the system. A professional inspection can identify and resolve the root cause.

While chemical drain cleaners can provide temporary relief, they may damage your pipes over time. It's safer to use a plunger or call a plumber to clear tough blockages.

First, turn off the main water supply to prevent further damage. Then, call an emergency plumbing service to handle the issue promptly and safely.

Unusual water bills, damp walls, mold growth, or the sound of running water when taps are off may indicate a hidden leak. A plumber can locate and fix it using leak detection tools.
